the connected log toward the final answer, one term at a time:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong>Sp</strong> — its similarity to the reasoning step that used it (its parent).</li>
<li><strong>Sgp</strong> — its similarity to the step above that (its grandparent).</li>
<li><strong>Relevancy</strong> — the average across those ancestors: did it stay on topic?</li>
<li><strong>Persistence</strong> — how many ancestors clear a threshold: did it <em>keep</em>
mattering, or spike once?</li>
<li><strong>Depth</strong> — how far it sits from the final answer: closer speaks louder.</li>
</ul>
<p>Normalize, weight, add:</p>
<pre><code>influence(d) = w1·relevancy + w2·persistence + w3·depth
</code></pre>
<p>One number per piece of evidence. And to be plain about what it is: every
term is a similarity proxy, cheap and deterministic — a ranking aid, not a
causal verdict. None of these terms is sacred. This is <strong>one way</strong> to score
influence, not the only way.</p>
